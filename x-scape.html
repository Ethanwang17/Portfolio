X-scape

Sept 2023 - Dec 2023

Our project, X-Scape, was crafted in response to the UN's Sustainable Development Goals, particularly Goal 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities. Tasked with addressing this imperative, we've developed a website dedicated to empowering users, notably UW students, to make eco-conscious transportation choices, thereby mitigating carbon emissions and fostering a more sustainable urban landscape.

1. Define

Problem

How might we inform young adult car buyers in King County about carbon emissions from various types of cars to help them minimize the environmental impact of their commutes?

What is Carbon Emissions?

• Carbon dioxide enters the atmosphere through burning fossil fuels (coal, natural gas, and oil)

• Fossil fuels – coal, oil and gas – are by far the largest contributor to global climate change, accounting for over 75 per cent of global greenhouse gas emissions and nearly 90 per cent of all carbon dioxide emissions

What is Global Warming?

• As greenhouse gas emissions blanket the Earth, they trap the sun’s heat. This leads to global warming and climate change

• The world is now warming faster than at any point in recorded history. Warmer temperatures over time are changing weather patterns and disrupting the usual balance of nature. This poses many risks to human beings and all other forms of life on Earth

Low-Fidelity

Using our design focus, my team and I created a low-fidelity wireframe to visualize our design. We used Figma to create our wireframe, and we used the wireframe to conduct user testing.

User Testing Findings

“The $$$ made it hard to grasp what the price of the car is”

Added a info hover icon that explains the range of prices for each $ sign

“Hard to search for a new car after already searching for one car”

Added a clear button in the search bar to easily restart and search for a new car

“The ‘More Info’ button was not clear it led to an external website”

Renamed button to “Visit Website” and also added an external link icon
